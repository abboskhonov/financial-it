Designer Demo Task — Financial IT Homepage
For: Designer candidate evaluation Time budget: 4–6 hours Project: financialit.net website revamp


1. Project Context
Financial IT (financialit.net) is a financial news and media platform serving ~50,000 monthly visitors. It publishes articles, video interviews, podcasts, webinars, and a digital magazine covering fintech, banking, payments, crypto, and related topics.

We are rebuilding the site from scratch. The goal is a faster, modern website that is easier to navigate, better on mobile, and feels premium without being corporate-stiff.

The new site must support advertising placements (standard IAB banners) integrated cleanly into the design — premium feel, not cluttered. It must also include a dark mode option.

Audience: Primary readers are fintech professionals, executives, and investors. They expect a publication that feels credible, modern, and serious — editorial quality akin to Bloomberg or Financial Times, but with the energy of a fintech-native brand.


2. Task Scope & Deliverables
Deliverable 1 — Homepage Desktop Mockup
Design the Financial IT homepage at desktop resolution (1440px).
Required Sections (top to bottom)
Header — Logo (left), main navigation menu with dropdowns, search icon, Login/Sign Up button, dark mode toggle
Leaderboard banner zone (728×90px) — clearly marked ad placement below header

3. Evaluation Criteria
Each submission is scored on a 0–10 scale across five dimensions:
Visual Craft (30% weight)
Typography hierarchy — are headings, body text, and metadata clearly differentiated?
Color usage — is the palette appropriate for a financial media brand? Not overly playful or excessively grim.
Spacing and proportion — does the layout breathe? Are sections clearly delineated?
Polish — attention to alignment, pixel-perfect spacing, consistent rounding.
Brand Fit (25% weight)
Credibility — does it feel like a publication you'd trust for fintech industry news?
Energy — does it avoid feeling like a generic corporate template?
Premium feel — are ads integrated cleanly, not making the page feel like a content farm?
Audience resonance — would a fintech executive find this site worth reading?
Layout & Information Architecture (20% weight)
Content hierarchy — is the hero clearly the most important thing on the page?
Scan-ability — can a reader quickly understand what's important?
Ad integration — do banner zones feel intentional and well-placed, not bolted on?
Flow — does the eye move naturally from section to section?
Style Guide Quality (15% weight)
Completeness — are all required elements covered?
Consistency — does the guide match the mockup? Do the guide's rules produce the same design?
Actionability — could a developer use this to build the site? Are hex codes, sizes, and spacing values specific?
Design Rationale (10% weight)
Include a brief paragraph (100–200 words) explaining key design decisions:

Why you chose the typeface
Why the color palette
What you prioritized given the time constraint

This is not judged on "correctness" but on whether the reasoning is coherent and user-centered.
